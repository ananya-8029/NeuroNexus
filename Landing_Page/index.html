The wonders of the world include both ancient and modern marvels. These iconic landmarks stand as testaments to the creativity, engineering prowess, and cultural significance of civilizations throughout history.
These were remarkable, humanly-constructed landmarks from ancient, classical civilisations.

The Seven Wonders of the World are a group of places around the globe that are considered to be of great importance. These are: The Colosseum in Italy, Petra in Jordan, Chichén Itzá in Mexico, Christ the Redeemer in Brazil, Machu Picchu in Peru, Taj Mahal in India and The Great Wall of China.

DID YOU KNOW!

The Great Wall of China

The original wall built under the rule of Emperor Qin Shi Huang of the Win Dynasty took around 20 years to finish. 21,196 kilometres long, the Great Wall witnesses highlands, landscapes, plateaus, natural barriers and more.

Christ the Redeemer, Brazil

Christ the Redeemer is a 125 feet tall statue designed by Heitor da Silva Costa, sculpted by French sculptor Paul Landowski. Since the statue is atop a mountain, it's prone to getting hit by lightning—in fact it gets hit around three to six times a year.

Petra, Jordan

A man made marvel, Petra is known as the Rose City, built out of pink sandstones. This structure, in fact is evidence that the Middle East was the most influential region of the world in the Middle Ages.

Machu Picchu, Peru

One of the most famous lost cities of the world, Machu Picchu, also known as the Lost City of the Incas, was built in 1450 and abandoned a hundred years after that.

Colosseum, Italy

Built between 70 AD and 82 AD by Emperor Titus Vespasian, the Colosseum in Rome is the most iconic, ancient amphitheatre in the world.

Chichen Itza, Mexico

Chichen Itza translates to “At the mouth of the well of Itza”. Believed to be the largest Mayan city ever built, the centre of the Chichen Itza comprises El Castillo, also known as the Temple of Kukulcan.

Taj Mahal, India

One of the most glorious displays of love in history is the Taj Mahal, built by emperor Shah Jahan in honour of his late wife.
